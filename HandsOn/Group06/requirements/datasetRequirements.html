Dataset requirements


R1: Data are in one of the selected smart city domains.

Yes.
The data is about public transportation, and in particular about the existing bicycle network within the City of Boston, with bike stations and trips.


R2: Data are available as a CSV file.

Yes.
The three datasets of interest are available in that format.


R3: Data have an open license so they can be published.

Yes.
The first 2 datasets have PPDL license. You are free:
• To Share: To copy, distribute and use the database.
• To Create: To produce works from the database.
• To Adapt: To modify, transform and build upon the database.
As long as you:
• Blank: This section is intentionally left blank. The PDDL imposes no restrictions on your use of the PDDL licensed database.
Source: https://opendatacommons.org/licenses/pddl/summary/

The license of the third dataset is more restrictive: Motivate hereby grants to you a non-exclusive, royalty-free, limited, perpetual license to access, reproduce, analyze, copy, modify, distribute in your product or service and use the Data for any lawful purpose (“License”).
Prohibited Conduct: Host, stream, publish, distribute, sublicense, or sell the Data as a stand-alone dataset; provided, however, you may include the Data as source material, as applicable, in analyses, reports, or studies published or distributed for non-commercial purposes.
However, as stated above, we are able to use the dataset as source material in our analysis.
Source: https://www.bluebikes.com/data-license-agreement.


R4: Data can be easily linked with generic real-world entities.

Yes.
It can be linked with different types of real-world entities: bikes, segments of the city, coordinates of bike's stations, streets, etc.

Moreover, some entities can be easily linked with entities in other dataset:
The street name (STREET_NAM in our datasets) can be linked with geographical location entries in famous geographocal datasets such as the one from OpenStreetMaps; the same can be said for the multiple other entities in our dataset that are related to geographical locations. Regarding entities in other datsets that could be linked with entities in our datasets, apart from the already mentioned geographical coordinates and information from OpenStreetMaps, we could find information regarding other less relevant entities linked with our domain given our information, such as, for example, the manufacturing details of the bike themselves (which is not considered of interest, in our context).


R5: Documentation exists for the data.

Yes.
Dataset1: Existing Bike Network
Source: https://data.boston.gov/dataset/existing-bike-network
Documentation: https://data.boston.gov/dataset/existing-bike-network-data-dictionary

Dataset2: Blue Bike Stations (locations)
Source: https://data.boston.gov/dataset/blue-bike-stations
Documentation: brief description on the same website.

Dataset 3: Blue Bikes System Data (trip histories)
Source: https://www.bluebikes.com/system-data
Documentation: https://www.bluebikes.com/system-data


R6: Data can come from multiple data sources.

Yes.
We observe that the data comes from two different sources.
The first two datasets are owned by the City of Boston and published by Boston Maps.
For the third one, Blue Bikes (formerly Hubway) is owned by the municipalities of Boston, Brookline, Cambridge and Somerville, and operated by Motivate International, Inc. This external website, maintained by Motivate International, Inc., provides datasets on Blue Bikes usage.
